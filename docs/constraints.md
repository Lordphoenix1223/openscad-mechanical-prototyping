# Constraints

## Functional Constraints

- Keep the bench-top mechanism small enough to print cheaply.
- Prefer parts that validate one assumption at a time.
- Separate cosmetic shell iteration from mechanism iteration.
- Design around M3 hardware because guessing fastener fit is a waste of time.

## Printability Constraints

- Avoid fake-thin walls.
- Prefer open slots and notches over enclosed tunnels that are harder to print and debug.
- Keep first paid prints small.
- Validate manifold geometry before trusting an STL.
- Check slicer-reported size and part count instead of trusting the viewport.

## Wearability Constraints

- Wider strap spacing reduces rotation.
- Visible clamp strategies are acceptable if they are mechanically honest.
- If the shell cannot mount securely, visual fidelity alone is not enough.

## Safety Constraints

- No projectile-focused design direction.
- No high-force testing.
- No claims of finished-device safety or reliability.
